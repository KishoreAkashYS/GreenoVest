## 3. IPCC Scenario Projection 
- **Selected IPCC Scenario:** SSP1-1.9
- **Scenario Brief:**  
  This scenario, titled "Sustainability – Taking the Green Road," represents the most ambitious IPCC pathway, designed to limit global warming to 1.5°C with no or limited overshoot.
  - **Temperature Target:** Limits the global mean temperature increase to below **1.5°C** above pre-industrial levels.
  - **Emissions Trajectory:** Requires global GHG emissions to peak **before 2025** and be reduced by **43%** below 2019 levels by 2030. This implies an annual linear reduction of at least **4.2%** for corporations.
  - **Net-Zero Milestone:** Global net-zero CO₂ emissions must be reached in the **early 2050s**.

## 4. Risk Assessment
- ### Major Emission Hotspots
    - **Scope 3:** The dominant source, accounting for **74% of total emissions in 2024**. Hotspots include purchased goods, capital goods (e.g., construction of data centers), and upstream transportation and distribution.
    - **Scope 1:** The fastest-growing category, having increased by **163% since 2019**. This is driven by the expansion of the company's delivery and logistics fleet and fossil fuel use in stationary operations.
    - Scope 2: While emissions have decreased due to renewable energy procurement, this area still requires vigilance as the company's electricity demand grows.


- ### Transition Risks 
  - **Regulatory Risk:** The company's large and growing absolute emissions footprint creates significant exposure to future **carbon pricing mechanisms**, compliance costs, and stricter emissions regulations across global markets.
  - **Reputational Risk:** There is a substantial and widening gap between the company's "net-zero by 2040" pledge and its actual performance of rising absolute emissions, risking damage to brand image and stakeholder trust.
  - **Operational Risk:** Decarbonization policies may impact key suppliers, potentially leading to **supply chain disruptions** or increased costs for materials and third-party logistics.
  - **Financial Risk:** Failure to demonstrate credible climate action can lead to **cost of capital escalation** as investors divest from companies misaligned with a 1.5°C pathway.

- ###  Financial Impact on Carbon Emissions
  While specific financial data was not provided for this analysis, the emissions gap represents a quantifiable financial risk. The 2024 emissions surplus of **27.83 million tCO₂e** above the 1.5°C pathway would translate into a significant financial liability under potential carbon pricing schemes. For example, at a conservative price of $50 per tonne, this gap represents a potential annual cost of over **$1.39 billion**. This liability is projected to grow substantially if the current trajectory continues, impacting profitability and shareholder value.



## 5. Opportunities for Decarbonization
- ### High-Impact Areas
    - **Aggressively decarbonize Scope 1** by **accelerating the electrification** of the entire global delivery and logistics fleet, moving well beyond current commitments.
    - Implement a **robust Scope 3 reduction strategy** by moving from encouragement to **mandating that top suppliers set their own 1.5°C-aligned Science-Based Targets**.
    - Focus on **low-carbon capital goods** by prioritizing green building materials and practices for all new data centers, warehouses, and fulfillment centers.

- ### Sectoral Benchmarks
    - The primary benchmark is the **Science-Based Targets initiative (SBTi)**, which requires an absolute emissions reduction of at least **4.2% per year** from a set baseline. The company's current performance, marked by a **33.4% absolute increase** since 2019, is in direct opposition to this benchmark.

- ### Technology & Innovation Levers
    - **Energy efficiency upgrades** in data centers and fulfillment centers to manage growing electricity demand.
    - Investment in and advocacy for **sustainable fuels** for hard-to-abate sectors like third-party aviation and maritime shipping.
    - Development and scaling of **circular economy initiatives** to reduce emissions from purchased goods, product use-phase, and end-of-life treatment.



## 6. Recommendations

The company is **Not Aligned** with the SSP1-1.9 scenario target. The gap between ambition and performance is significant and growing.

Recommendations to achieve alignment with the selected IPCC scenario:

- **Shift focus to absolute emission reductions** across all corporate strategy, reporting, and executive compensation, moving beyond insufficient carbon intensity metrics.
- **Accelerate Scope 1 decarbonization programs**, particularly the **full electrification of the last-mile delivery fleet** and the transition away from fossil fuels for heating and power in all facilities.
- Implement a **transformative supply chain engagement program** that **mandates top suppliers to set their own SBTi-aligned targets** and provides incentives for decarbonization.
- Formally set and validate **near-term (5-10 year) absolute emission reduction targets** with the Science Based Targets initiative (SBTi) to provide a credible and transparent roadmap.
- **Re-evaluate business growth models** to actively **decouple revenue growth from emissions growth**, particularly in physical operations and logistics.



## 7. Conclusion
- The company’s current emissions profile is **not aligned** with the **SSP1-1.9** trajectory.
- Alignment status is determined based on the significant and growing emissions gap, with absolute emissions increasing rather than decreasing as required by climate science.
- To **achieve** alignment, the company must focus on:
  - **Key Action 1:** **Aggressively reducing absolute Scope 1 emissions** through rapid fleet electrification.
  - **Key Action 2:** Implementing a **mandatory supplier decarbonization program** to address the dominant Scope 3 emissions.
  - **Key Action 3:** Setting and validating **near-term, absolute emission reduction targets** with SBTi to ensure accountability and credibility.

**Sustained commitment and proactive transformation are critical for climate resilience and competitive advantage.**
